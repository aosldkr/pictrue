Medieval Art

The period from the 4th to the 14th centuries BC was an absolute period of Christian value. Symbolic and supernatural art and church architecture based on religious content centered on Christianity are typical, and this period is viewed as an art and culture and artistic dark period (more church rules and values than individual thoughts or painting styles). Medieval art can be seen as Byzantine, Romanesque, and Gothic art.

Gothic Art

Gothic art, or Gothic art, is a medieval art movement that developed in northern France in the late 12th century following Romanesque art.Since its first popularity in France, it has spread throughout Western Europe and the northern Alps, and has become a representative art style of medieval Europe. Despite regional differences, the Gothic art movement continued into the 15th century and led to the Renaissance.Gothic painting, along with the development of realistic trends, was transformed into a decline in pure crafts, but copy was prevalent mainly in Paris in the 13th century.
