Renaissance Art

Art trends that began in Italy in the 14th century and peaked throughout Europe in the 16th century. Originally, the term Renaissance, which means "rebirth" in French, encompasses a transition period to mark the end of the middle century and the start of the modern era, in addition to a specific era in the literary and artistic movements.The Renaissance basically meant a cultural and artistic revival, especially a conscious movement to revive Greek and Roman culture.As they returned to Florence and began their work using their classical knowledge, the art of the ancient world came to light again.

Baroque Art

Baroque art focuses on capturing dynamic forms, maximizing the contrast between light and darkness, and emphasizes balance through harmony of parts that depend on the whole, and the balance is made up of perfect parts.I can't compare it to the overall balance of the High Renaissance, but it was closer to the balance of the Renaissance than the deliberate dissonance of mannerism or the fragmentation of Rococo. In the case of painting, the overall characteristics include diagonal composition, perspective, shortening, and the use of eye-cheating effects.

It is common with Baroque in that it dislikes straight lines and loves curved, curved, elaborate decorations. compared to the powerful latter, Rococo is rather elegant and light, with S-shaped curves, non-symmetrical decorations, and an exotic atmosphere, especially the Chinese style.
