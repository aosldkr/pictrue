Louvre In Paris

The Louvre is a national museum in the city of Rivoli, central Paris, France. It is one of the world's leading museums along with the Metropolitan Museum of Art and the British Museum in terms of the number and quality of its collections. The current building is a renovation of the Louvre Palace and is designated as a World Heritage Site, including around the Seine River in Paris. It's a glass pyramid sculpture in front of the Louvre. The Louvre opened its doors on August 10, 1793, displaying 537 paintings, mostly collected from fallen aristocrats and churches. Due to structural problems inside the museum building, the museum was closed from 1796 to 1801. During Napoleon's reign, the collection greatly increased in size and changed its name from Waterloo to Napoleon.

Moma New York

The Museum of Modern Art is a museum dedicated to modern and contemporary art in New York City. It is a modern art museum with a strong influence in the world, and is also called MoMA because it takes the initials of the English name.The Moma Collection provides a broad overview of modern and contemporary art, including architecture, design, drawing, painting, installation, photography, publications, book and electronic media including art books, movies and games. The MoMA Library contains approximately 300,000 books and exhibition catalogs, more than 1,000 periodicals,It has more than 40,000 short-lived materials (documents, promotional materials, tickets, brochures, receipts, etc.) related to individual and group artists. Archival holds various materials related to the history of modern and contemporary art.
